TO MY GIRL, CHERRYL

At a bus stop that wasn’t meant to matter, time forgot its job. Cars passed, people came and went, but we stayed like the world had paused just for us. You laughed that day… not the quiet kind people fake, but the kind that spills over, that makes strangers glance twice, that makes moments permanent. I didn’t know then that I’d carry that sound with me through silence, through distance, even through the space where we stopped speaking. Two years can change a lot, but somehow, that memory stayed untouched still warm, still loud, still yours. And now we’re here again, writing new sentences on top of old feelings, but I still remember how you looked at me that day… like nothing else mattered. Maybe that’s what you are not just a person, but a pause in time I never really left. I LOVE YOU SO MUCH💖.

There’s something about you—soft, quiet, but impossible to ignore The way you smile, the way you stay in my mind… it’s different And somewhere along the way, you became special to me

— from Billings
